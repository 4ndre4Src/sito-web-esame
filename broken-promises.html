Presented as a dynamic, shared universe, No Man's Sky promised player interactions, complex ecosystems, and a nearly infinite variety of truly unique planets. At launch, many of these features were absent or severely simplified: multiplayer was nonexistent, creatures and environments were repetitive, and the systemic mechanics were shallower than expected. The game underwent extensive updates in the years that followed, but the gap between promise and initial release remained one of the most emblematic cases of overcommunication in the industry.

Revealed at E3 2012 as a new graphical frontier for open-world gaming, Watch Dogs impressed with its advanced lighting, urban density, and systemic interactions. However, upon release, the game featured a noticeable visual downgrade compared to the original demo, with a less responsive city and reduced AI systems. This gap between the presentation and the final product fueled one of the first major discussions about the topic of "downgrade" in the new console era.

REALITY

Announced as a revolutionary and deeply immersive RPG, Cyberpunk 2077 promised a responsive world, complex systems, and solid performance across all platforms. At launch, especially on old-gen consoles, the game was plagued by serious technical issues, widespread bugs, and limited AI. Many of the promises regarding depth of gameplay and urban simulation failed to fully materialize, making the launch one of the most controversial in the medium's recent history.
